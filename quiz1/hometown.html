About Hometown

Surabaya City of Heroes

Surabaya, often referred to as the "City of Heroes," holds a special place in Indonesian history due to its pivotal role in the nation's struggle for independence. This vibrant metropolis, located in East Java, earned its heroic reputation during the Battle of Surabaya in November 1945, a fierce and valiant resistance against Dutch colonial forces. The resilience and determination of Surabaya's residents in the face of adversity have forever etched the city's name into the annals of Indonesia's fight for freedom. Today, Surabaya stands as a bustling, modern city that seamlessly blends its rich historical heritage with contemporary progress. Visitors can explore the Tugu Pahlawan, a towering monument dedicated to the heroes of Indonesia, and the House of Sampoerna, a museum that provides insights into the country's tobacco industry history. The city's vibrant street art, bustling markets, and diverse culinary scene further showcase its dynamic spirit. Surabaya remains a beacon of strength and resilience, embodying the indomitable spirit of its heroic past.
